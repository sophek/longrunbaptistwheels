AMERICA: JOSEPH'S EGYPT FADING Revelation 3:8 I know thy works: behold, I HAVE SET BEFORE THEE AN OPEN DOOR, and no man can shut it: for thou hast a little strength, and hast kept my word, and hast not denied my name. Genesis 47:3 And Pharaoh said unto his brethren, What is your occupation? These prophecies are found presented in the Old Testament in both a literal and typical manner. For example, it should be obvious that Daniel in the Lion's Den (and his subsequent restoration and exaltation) is a picture of "Jacob's Trouble" in the coming Tribulation Period, followed by the regathering and receiving of the Jews as predicted in Romans 11, Zechariah 12, etc. We also see in Daniel a picture of the death, burial, resurrection and exaltation of our Lord. But it would be a dry, stale interpreter, indeed, that could not also find devotional teachings and illustrations for modern Christians (in this age) in the Old Testament: Romans 15:4 For whatsoever things were WRITTEN AFORETIME were written for our learning, that we through patience and comfort of the scriptures might have hope. 2 Timothy 3:16 ALL SCRIPTURE is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness: 17 That the man of God may be perfect, throughly furnished unto all good works. 1 Corinthians 10:11 Now all these things happened unto them for ensamples: and they are written for our admonition, upon whom the ends of the world are come. The Old Testament reveals to us the ways of God in regard to His people. God does not change in regard to His holiness, perfection, etc. Therefore, it is no wonder, that although we are in a new dispensation, and there are some changes in this age of God's grace and patience, there is also much that is the same. For example, Godâ€™s dealings with Gentile nations and cities in the Old Testament was not much different than how He deals with them today. Things are perhaps a bit more complicated, since in the midst of the nations today, we have both natural Israel in blindness and judgment, as well as the spiritual family of God (i.e. Christians, both Jews and Gentiles). Christians are now the Lordâ€™s lightbearers in the world, called to shine forth His truth and righteousness. We are called to be pilgrims or sojourners, passing through this brief, wicked age in patience and holiness. And this brings us to the above verses in Genesis 47. Jacob and his sons were suffering under a terrible famine. After the revelation of Joseph, they were called to Egypt where they were nourished and preserved. Egypt is, therefore, both a type of the world and its bondage, as well as a means of Godâ€™s preservation in the midst of trials, famine, etc. Our Lord Himself was temporarily preserved as a child in Egypt: Matthew 2:13 And when they were departed, behold, the angel of the Lord appeareth to Joseph in a dream, saying, Arise, and take the young child and his mother, and flee into EGYPT, and be thou there until I bring thee word: for Herod will seek the young child to destroy him. Joseph's brethren confessed to Pharaoh that they were only "sojourners" in Egypt (47:4). By God's wonderful, amazing Providence, they receive the best of the land in Egypt for their FLOCKS, and they are preserved from the worldwide famine. The same God who preserved and blessed His people in the Old Testament is still alive in this age of grace. In the midst of persecution, famine and trial, He led our forefathers, and their flocks, to a new land in America. For example, Baptists, who had been persecuted in every other land, began to THRIVE (along with other, American Christians) after the American Revolution. Just as Joseph's brethren petitioned Pharaoh for Goshen, these early American Christians secured the First Amendment through their importunate prayers to God, and importunate petitions to civil leaders. The provincial Congress of 1774 declared on December 9th: "On reading of the memorial of the Rev. Isaac Backus, agent of to the Baptist Churches in this government, 'Resolved, that the establishment of civil and religious liberty to each denomination in the province is the sincere wish of this Congress; but being by no means vested with the powers of civil governmentâ€¦they therefore recommend to the Baptist Churches that when a General Assembly shall be covened in this Colony, they lay the real grievances of said churches before the same; when and where their petition will most certainly meet with all the attention due to the memorial of a denomination of Christians so well disposed to the public weal of their country - by order of Congress, John Hancock." Subsequent events pushed the issue aside until an address was presented to Washington in 1789: "In August, 1789, therefore a committee of the Baptist churches in Virginia presented an address to General Washingtonâ€¦wherein they expressedâ€¦'a fear that our RELIGIOUS RIGHTS were not well secured in our new Constitution of government.' In answer to this he assured them of his readiness to use his influence to make these rights indispensableâ€¦In the following month, accordingly, an amendment to the Constitution was passed declaring that Congress should 'make no law respecting any establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof.'" (Curtis, "Progress of Baptist Principles," 1855) It is for these reasons (and others) that the Baptists, long persecuted and depised, were nevertheless, soon "standing before Pharaoh," reaping the praise of scholars and historians as the chief defenders of religious liberty!: "Freedom of conscience, unlimited freedom of mind, was from the first the trophy of the Baptists." (George Bancroft, "History of the United States," 1840) "The Baptists were the first propounders of absolute liberty, just and true liberty, equal and impartial liberty." (John Locke) As Joseph, after a life of sore persecution, was exalted in Egypt to be a blessing to the entire world, so were Baptists, for the first time in history, permitted to thrive unmolested. Christianity (especially Bible Christianity) prospered abundantly. Like Israel, Baptists were given a place for their FLOCKS to thrive in America, and they began to multiply exceedingly: Genesis 47:27 And Israel dwelt in the land of Egypt, in the country of GOSHEN; and they had possessions therein, and grew, and MULTIPLIED EXCEEDINGLY. Baptists (who had almost no churches in the South in the 18th century), soon became the largest denomination in America at the start of the 19th century! They were very influential. We should not miss this picture. The Egyptians (with Joseph in their midst) had never fully repented of idolatry. The whole land never fully adopted the Hebrew religion. The pagan, Egyptian priests were still in the land, enjoying the physical blessings wrought by Joseph's wisdom: Genesis 47:26 And Joseph made it a law over the land of Egypt unto this day, that Pharaoh should have the fifth part; except the land of the PRIESTS only, which became not Pharaoh's. But the amazing thing is that Pharaoh and the Egyptians were MIGHTILY INFLUENCED by Joseph and his God. They did not owe their physical salvation (from the famine) to any Egyptian deity, AND THEY KNEW IT! What a precious parallel this is. It cannot be denied that many of the famous forefathers of this country were Masons, Deists, etc. (e.g. Washington was a Mason, and Franklin was both a Mason and a Deist). But, like Pharoah, and much of Egypt, they had experienced miraculous blessings from the God of the Bible. Many of the Founding Fathers were truly converted. And those who were not, nevertheless were quick to confess their debt to Godâ€™s Providence. By "God" they meant the God of the Bible. Although Joseph was probably surrounded with images of Horus (the falcon-headed, Egyptian god), and his eye, (Eye of Horus), the INFLUENCE Joseph had upon Egypt and its leaders was God-ordained and miraculous! In the same manner, whatever pagan forces were at work when America was founded, and whatever pagan or Masonic symbols were utilized (e.g. the Great Seal, etc.), it should not be denied that God ordained America to be a GOSHEN to Christians. Like Egypt under Joseph, America soon became a miraculous "open door" to spread the "BREAD OF LIFE" into all the FAMINED lands: Genesis 41:55 And when all the land of Egypt was famished, the people cried to Pharaoh FOR BREAD: and Pharaoh said unto all the Egyptians, Go unto Joseph; what he saith to you, do. 56 And the famine was over all the face of the earth: And Joseph opened all the storehouses, and sold unto the Egyptians; and the famine waxed sore in the land of Egypt. 57 And all countries came into Egypt to Joseph for to buy corn; because that the famine was so sore in all lands. Egypt was not able to provide this bread through its own power or wisdom. It was only able to be a blessing to the whole world BECAUSE OF JOSEPH (who was simply an instrument of God). How could it be that Hebrew shepherds could one day thrive in Egypt? Shepherds were an abomination to the Egyptians: Genesis 46:33 And it shall come to pass, when Pharaoh shall call you, and shall say, What is your occupation? 34 That ye shall say, Thy servants' trade hath been about cattle from our youth even until now, both we, and also our fathers: that ye may dwell in the land of Goshen; for EVERY SHEPHERD IS AN ABOMINATION UNTO THE EGYPTIANS. Nevertheless, God was able to ordain things in such a manner that these shepherds were given the BEST land of Egyptian in which to prosper in, and multiply. As Egypt was used as an instrument to protect the men of Israel, Egypt was also, in turn, greatly blessed by Joseph and Jacob. Without Joseph, Egypt would have starved to death in the famine. Jacob also "blessed" Pharaoh: Genesis 47:10 And Jacob BLESSED Pharaoh, and went out from before Pharaoh. Now notice the parallels with America. Is it not obvious that any indepth studies of Egyptian paganism, in order to prove that GOSHEN was a PAGAN kingdom, would be greatly beside the point? Goshen was a GOD-ORDAINED land of nourishment and protection for God's people in the midst of famine. The pagans were used as instruments, and instead of the paganism influencing Jacob and his sons, the reverse was, in fact, the truth (at least at first). By God's power, Pharaoh had to let go of the reins! He let Joseph drive, and whatever Joseph, with his Divine wisdom, wanted, he received. The land of Egypt was temporarily, indirectly ruled by a small band of Hebrews who had the true religion. In the same manner, any indepth studies of early America, in order to prove that America was founded by occult forces and pagan Masons, etc., is greatly beside the point. God used a gathering of Christians, Deists, Masons (and who knows what else?) to form a country that was as much aware of its dependance upon the God of the CHRISTIAN religion, as Pharaoh and all of Egypt was aware that they were dependant upon Joseph and his Hebrew God! The speeches, the prayers, the documents, the letters, the public sermons in early America, all reveal a nation deeply impressed and aware of its dependance upon the God of the Bible. Who could have imagined that such a thing could transpire? Joseph as the leader of Egypt? Jacob and his shepherd-sons given royal treatment and privileges in the mightiest kingdom on earth? And likewise, who could have imagined BAPTISTS in early America being blessed and protected to such a degree that they soon MULTIPLY and become the largest Christian sect (in the 19th century)? Based on this typical analogy, let us now notice the tension that must be held between two extreme views. Jacob and his sons confessed that they were only "sojourners" in the land (Genesis 47:4). Jacob made Joseph sware that he would not bury him permanently in Egypt: Genesis 47:29 And the time drew nigh that Israel must die: and he called his son Joseph, and said unto him, If now I have found grace in thy sight, put, I pray thee, thy hand under my thigh, and deal kindly and truly with me; bury me not, I pray thee, in Egypt: 30 But I will lie with my fathers, and thou shalt carry me out of Egypt, and bury me in their buryingplace. And he said, I will do as thou hast said. And Joseph, likewise, made a similar demand upon the children of Israel: Genesis 50:24 And Joseph said unto his brethren, I die: and God will surely visit you, and bring you out of this land unto the land which he sware to Abraham, to Isaac, and to Jacob. 25 And Joseph took an oath of the children of Israel, saying, God will surely visit you, and ye shall carry up my bones from hence. Therefore, although Egypt was a great blessing to Jacob and his sons, and although it was greatly used in God's Providence, Israel in these early days of prosperity never lost the greater hope! Is it not amazing that the "glory days" of premillennialism and hope in the Second Coming, coincided with America's independence and prosperity? In this we see our first warning and lesson. What if Jacobâ€™s descendants would have started seeing Egypt as "the land of promise"? What if they would have forgotten the promises and the prophecies? Did God intend Egypt and Goshen to be the fulfillment of His promises to Abraham, Isaac and Jacob? Certainly not! Did God intend them to lose sight of their future and "settle down" in Egypt? Certainly not. In this we see the post-millennial, dominionist errors, the old Roman Catholic error, and the preterist error. These teachings focus on THIS world to such an extent that they would have Jacob and their sons abandoning their shepherding occupation in order to â€œintermingleâ€ among the Egyptians. They would tell Jacob and his sons, "Dominion is your obligation. Egypt, in the present, is your ultimate goal. Intermingle among the Egyptians and rule them. Egypt is the fulfillment of the prophecies to your forefathers. Cast off the pilgrim spirit! It is too negative." In other words, this erroneous view makes TOO MUCH of America. It is patriotism perverted into idolatry. Samuel Davies Baldwin, a Methodist minister, in 1858, was an example of this extreme view concerning America: "Our country is true and faithful. It has never enacted a single law unauthorized by the DIVINE constitution; nor have the decisions of our supreme judiciary ever been counter to the revealed legislation of heaven." ("Dominion; or, the Unity and Trinity of the Human Race," 1858) This is the view that is blind to the evils that run rampant in a nation and its government. It is very gullible. It ignores the prophecies. There is, however, an extreme view on the other side. It would tell Jacob and his sons, "Stay out of Egypt. If you must go, do not appear before Pharaoh. Rebuke Joseph. This whole journey down to Egypt is a plan created by Satan. The bread of Egypt is poison. It is a trick. Pharaoh is wicked, and we should have no dealings whatsoever with him or the Egyptians." But this view would not be in harmony with Scripture: Genesis 45:5 Now therefore be not grieved, nor angry with yourselves, that ye sold me hither: for God did send me before you to preserve life. 7 And God sent me before you to preserve you a posterity in the earth, and to save your lives by a great deliverance. 8 So now it was not you that sent me hither, but God: and he hath made me a father to Pharaoh, and lord of all his house, and a ruler throughout all the land of Egypt. Genesis 50:20 But as for you, ye thought evil against me; but God meant it unto good, to bring to pass, as it is this day, to save much people alive. 21 Now therefore fear ye not: I will nourish you, and your little ones. And he comforted them, and spake kindly unto them. In other words, this view would totally miss the wonderful Providence of God, in turning the heart of Pharaoh towards Israel, and sustaining God's people. In regard to America, this view would see the whole founding of the country as a Satanic conspiracy. It would scoff at the open door of opportunities. It would say, "See, there is a Pharaoh is coming that will not even remember Joseph. Therefore, Joseph should have never went down to Egypt. He should have let his family starve to death in the Promised Land. He should have let the whole world starve. God is in control. If God wants people to be fed, He will take care of it. If God wants babies delivered from abortion, He will do it. If God wants souls delivered from eternal damnation, He will take care of it. America has been a terrible mistake. Let's just stick our heads in a hole, live for pleasure, eat, drink, and be merry, and keep everything easy-going as the world starves." No, God has always used kings and leaders for His wonderful purposes IN SPITE of their evil intentions (real or imagined): Proverbs 21:1 The king's heart is in the hand of the LORD, as the rivers of water: he turneth it whithersoever he will. On this 4th of July, 2004, we must therefore be thankful that the Lord has raised up America, and has allowed an open door of religious liberty in order for His people to multiply and prosper, and raise up their "flocks." We must certainly never lose sight of the coming of the Lord and HIS Kingdom. We must never forget that we are called to be sojourners: 1 Peter 1:17 And if ye call on the Father, who without respect of persons judgeth according to every man's work, pass the time of YOUR SOJOURNING HERE in fear: 1 Peter 2:11 Dearly beloved, I beseech you as STRANGERS AND PILGRIMS, abstain from fleshly lusts, which war against the soul; But we should recognize God's hand of Providence in our country and be thankful for it. We must tell our children the great things God did in this country. We must remember the amazing, wonderful miracles of His Providence that brought so many leaders and people to confess their faith, dependance and gratitude to the God of the Bible, and therefore found their nation on Christian principles. We must not allow this incredible history to be altered, denied or perverted. But we also must not be carried away into a false idealism, a hyper-patriotism that is unbiblical. We must not lose sight of the prophecies and the promises. Egypt under Pharaoh (even with the blessings of Joseph) is NOT the Promised Land. We must remember the END of the story: Exodus 1:6 And Joseph died, and all his brethren, and all that generation. 7 And the children of Israel were fruitful, and increased abundantly, and multiplied, and waxed exceeding mighty; and the land was filled with them. 8 Now THERE AROSE UP A NEW KING OVER EGYPT, WHICH KNEW NOT JOSEPH. 12 But the more they afflicted them, the more they multiplied and grew. And they were grieved because of the children of Israel. 13 And the Egyptians made the children of Israel to serve with rigour: Jacob stayed in Goshen - "in" Egypt, but not really "of" it. As Christians, we must also keep ourselves separate (2 Corinthians 6) in a Biblical manner, without retreating into laziness, burying our talents into the earth, hiding our light, letting the salt lose its savour. Let us pray and strive for the Biblical balance. Let us avoid, on one side, the extremes of Preterism, Roman Catholicism and Dominion/Post Millennial theology, and on the other side, the view of a few dispensationalists that since the whole world ends in apostasy and the Antichrist, any attempt to do anything at all to better people is sin. But our Lord went about doing good, WHILE He preached the Gospel, and called people to hope in the world to come: Acts 10:38 How God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Ghost and with power: who WENT ABOUT DOING GOOD, and healing all that were oppressed of the devil; for God was with him. Let us pray for or country, and do what we can to take advantage of the liberties that are still left.
