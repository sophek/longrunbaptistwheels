THE TEN COMMANDMENTS OF LIBERTINISM - KAU#198

THE TEN COMMANDMENTS OF LIBERTINISM The Bible clearly teaches that Christendom, in the latter days, will be invaded by a mystical, Gnostic libertinism: 2 Peter 2:19 While they promise them LIBERTY, they themselves are the servants of corruption: for of whom a man is overcome, of the same is he brought in bondage. 2 Timothy 4:3 For the time will come when they will not endure sound doctrine; but after their own lusts shall they heap to themselves teachers, having itching ears; 4 And they shall turn away their ears from the truth, and shall be turned unto fables. 2 Peter 3:16 As also in all his [Paul's] epistles, speaking in them of these things; in which are some things hard to be understood, which they that are unlearned and unstable WREST, as they do also the other scriptures, unto their own destruction. 17 Ye therefore, beloved, seeing ye know these things before, beware lest ye also, being led away with the error of the wicked, FALL from your own stedfastness. Jude 1:4 For there are certain men crept in unawares, who were before of old ordained to this condemnation, ungodly men, TURNING the grace of our God INTO LASCIVIOUSNESS, and denying the only Lord God, and our Lord Jesus Christ. It has now arrived, and it is gaining ground. The blight is thoroughly ecumenical. It is found in every denomination. In varying degrees, it has also made inroads into fundamentalism and conservative evangelicalism. It is time for every Christian leader to boldly take a stand against this creeping fungus. Libertinism In History In defining "Gnosticism," John Henry Blunt's "Dictionary of Sects" (1903), states: "Several sects of the [early Gnostics] were clearly antinomian....their theory of the Demiurge was soon extended from the sphere of physical creation to that of MORAL LAW, and then the moral law shared with the material world in condemnation as his evil work. Thus a theoretical Antinomianism sprung up which soon developed into practical license..."(1) Irenaeus (c. 130-202), in the 2nd century, rebuked the doctrines and practices of these early, libertine heretics: "....they think the spiritual is incapable of receiving corruption, whatever moral conduct they may practice....they say of themselves, whatever may be the character of their material morality, that they cannot be injured by it, nor lose their spiritual substance. Hence the most perfect among them perform all forbidden things without any scruple."(2) He continues: "Wherefore also it comes to pass, that the 'most perfect' among them addict themselves WITHOUT FEAR to all those kinds of forbidden deeds of which the Scriptures assure us that 'they who do such things shall not inherit the kingdom of God.'....at every heathen festival celebrated in honour of the idols, these men are the first to assemble; and to such a pitch do they go, that some of them DO NOT EVEN keep away from that bloody spectacle hateful both to God and men, in which gladiators either fight with wild beasts, or singly encounter one another....And committing many other abominations and impieties, they run US down (WHO FROM THE FEAR OF GOD guard against sinning even in thought or word) as utterly contemptible and ignorant persons, while they highly exalt themselves...."(3) Obviously, Irenaeus, if he were living today, would find that the libertine Gnosticism he wrote against is now engulfing much of modern Christendom in its hellish flames! The "bloody spectacle hateful both to God and men" is blaring, with gory close-ups, in the very living rooms of almost every Christian. And to speak of the "fear of God" today is to invite scorn and rage, if not a literal stoning. At the time of the Reformation (and afterwards), multiple sects would arise advocating evil, antinomian principles. These immoral sects should not be confused with the obedient Christians in history who proclaimed the true Gospel of God. Many of these saints were, like the apostle Paul (Romans 3:8), slandered by Romanists and Legalists as "antinomian" because they made a proper distinction between positional justification and practical sanctification, and taught eternal salvation by grace through faith alone.(4) Yet, some of the various sects that were called "antinomian," deserved the name. The "Libertines" were one of the first immoral groups to arise during the Reformation. According to the "Concise Encyclopedia of Religious Knowledge" (1895), the Libertines were pantheistic: "A party of loose pantheistic-rationalists which arose in the Netherlands during the Reformation. They affirmed that nothing was bad by itself and that God is all, and all is God, so that the natural passions are but the voices of the Spirit."(5) Blunt's "Dictionary of Sects" writes concerning the Libertines: "Their principles were, that God being the Author of all human actions, no human action could be evil; that religion consists in union with God by contemplation, and that any one who had attained to this was free to act according to their pleasure."(6) The Ten Libertine Commandments It is obvious that "libertinism" (past and present) is the justification of SIN by perverting Scripture or reason. The following are some of its commandments that the devils have given to Christendom through false teachers over the years: I. "Since the body is material, and not spiritual, always believe and preach that God does not care what we do in the body." Refuted: Romans 12:1 I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that ye present your BODIES a living sacrifice, HOLY, acceptable unto God, which is your reasonable service. II. "Since the Pharisees served God outwardly, but were sinners inwardly, always believe and preach that God does not care about the outward." Refuted: We are called to have a higher moral righteousness than the Pharisees, by having an outward AND an inward morality through Jesus Christ (Matthew 23:23): Matthew 5:20 For I say unto you, That except your righteousness shall EXCEED the righteousness of the scribes and Pharisees, ye shall in no case enter into the kingdom of heaven. Jesus does not teach that the outward is not important!: "....is therefore Christ the minister of sin? God forbid." (Galatians 2:17) Indeed, some works should remain private (devotions, fasting, alms). Yet, "private" does not mean INWARD. And many other works are to be performed outwardly in the sight of men: Matthew 5:16 Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven. III. "The spirit of every Christian is perfect. Therefore, the thoughts and impressions of every Christian are one with God and His Spirit. Always believe and preach that Christians do not have to worry about sinning in spirit." Refuted: This lie not only "sanctifies" inward sins such as pride, envy, hatred, etc. It also causes Christians to presumptuously think (before testing) that their decisions and desires are given by God, when they may be from the Devil. The whole man (body, soul and spirit) of a Christian is eternally saved (imputed righteousness). Yet, none are totally sanctified in this life. The goal of the Christian life is to sanctify the whole man (outward and inward) practically. The libertines leave out one or the other, either by teaching that it is already 100% sanctified, or by teaching that it doesn't matter in the eyes of God. But God wills that all of the Christian (body, soul, spirit) is not yet perfect, but should be growing toward perfection: 1 Corinthians 5:5 To deliver such an one unto Satan for the destruction of the flesh, that the SPIRIT may be saved in the day of the Lord Jesus. Colossians 3:2 Set your AFFECTION on things above, not on things on the earth. 1 Thessalonians 5:23 And the very God of peace sanctify you WHOLLY; and I pray God your whole SPIRIT and SOUL and BODY be preserved BLAMELESS unto the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ. 1 Corinthians 6:20 For ye are bought with a price: therefore glorify God in your body, and in your SPIRIT, which are God's. 1 Thessalonians 3:13 To the end he may stablish your HEARTS UNBLAMEABLE IN HOLINESS before God, even our Father, at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ with all his saints. Ephesians 4:23 And be renewed in the SPIRIT of your mind; IV. "I AM sinning outwardly, but this is simply my 'flesh.' Paul says that the flesh is evil and it cannot do any good. Always believe and preach this." Refuted: This lie confuses the TEMPTATION to evil within a man (which will remain until glory), with the Christian himself! When the Christian sins, it is the Christian himself that has WALKED AFTER or SOWN to the flesh (the inward temptations): 2 Peter 2:10 But chiefly them that WALK AFTER THE FLESH in the lust of uncleanness.... Romans 8:1 There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus, who WALK NOT after the FLESH, but after the Spirit. Galatians 6:8 For he that SOWETH to his flesh shall of the flesh reap corruption... It is not the "flesh" that soweth to the flesh! It is not the "flesh" that walks after the flesh! It is the Christian himself. Therefore, blaming sin on the flesh will get the Christian nowhere at the Judgment Seat of Christ. Every Christian is indeed positionally justified. But we are not discussing imputed righteousness. We are discussing the Christian WALK. And to excuse or blame sin in the Christian life on the "flesh" is irresponsible and antinomian. V. "When God sees me, He only sees Jesus. Always believe and preach that there will be no real chastisement for sin at the Judgment Seat because of this truth." Refuted: Malachi 2:17 Ye have wearied the LORD with your words. Yet ye say, Wherein have we wearied him? When ye say, Every one that DOETH EVIL is good in the sight of the LORD, and he delighteth in them; or, Where is the God of judgment? This statement is true in regard to eternal salvation. But God did not justify the sinner just to leave him in sin. The new Christian is now commanded to use the Word of God, and the indwelling Spirit, and his new privileges and motivations to GROW. In regard to the Christian walk, the Christian is only HOLY if he is WALKING in holiness. If he sows to the flesh, he will reap corruption and temporary destruction (1 Corinthians 3:16, 2 Corinthians 5:9-11, Galatians 6:8). VI. "Obeying commandments is fleshly. We should simply love God. Always believe and preach this." Refuted: This lie divorces the love of God from obedience to God, and turns the Christian life into a mystical, contemplative antinomianism. The Pharisees sinned by professing to love God through outward works in the sight of men. Their motives were wrong. Inwardly, they were full of hatred, pride, greed and envy (Matthew 23:28). Therefore, the Pharisees were not "fleshly" because they were obeying God's commandments! They were fleshly for only obeying SOME of them! The true love of God must strive to obey ALL of God's commandments (and confess when we fail): 1 John 5:3 For this is the love of God, that we keep his commandments: and his commandments are not grievous. John 15:10 If ye keep my commandments, ye shall abide in my love; even as I have kept my Father's commandments, and abide in his love. John 14:15 If ye love me, keep my commandments. 1 Corinthians 7:19 Circumcision is nothing, and uncircumcision is nothing, but the keeping of the commandments of God. These verses shatter the lies of the new libertine Gnosticism that has infected the churches. VII. "Since no one is perfect, I have to WALK in sin. Always believe and preach this." Refuted: Luke 1:6 And they were both righteous before God, walking in all the commandments and ordinances of the Lord blameless. 2 Corinthians 1:12 For our rejoicing is this, the testimony of our conscience, that in simplicity and godly sincerity, not with fleshly wisdom, but by the grace of God, we have had our conversation in the world, and more abundantly to you-ward. John 21:17 ...Lord, thou knowest all things; thou knowest that I love thee. Jesus saith unto him, Feed my sheep. There is obviously a degree of relative perfection (based on each man's light, maturity, degree of temptations, etc.) that we can walk in. Paul, in Romans 7, is discussing the inward principle of fleshly temptation that he found to be inescapable. Nowhere in the chapter does he maintain that the Christian must WALK after this inward law of sin. To do so is to be in danger of judgment. When we walk after sin, we should fear God and confess it, and forsake sin in repentance. But it is a worse sin to excuse it by saying that we HAVE to walk after it: Jeremiah 18:12 And they said, There is no hope: but we will WALK after our own devices, and we will every one DO the imagination of his evil heart. VIII. "Since God is Sovereign, all of my sin is caused by God willing me to sin. Always believe and preach this." Refuted: God can cause the wrath of man to praise him, and use man's sin to work out His own purposes, as in the case of Joseph's brethren, or Nebuchadnezzar's chastisement upon Israel. Yet, the Babylonian kings were later punished for their SIN (Jeremiah 25:12). Sin is disobedience to God. It is DOUBLE sin to then blame God for disobedience: Galatians 2:17 ....is therefore Christ the minister of sin? God forbid. 1 John 3:8 He that committeth sin is of the devil... Ezekiel 33:11 Say unto them, As I live, saith the Lord GOD, I have no pleasure in the death of the wicked; but that the wicked turn from his way and live: turn ye, turn ye from your evil ways; for why will ye die, O house of Israel? IX. "This IS sin. But it is not ME. My new nature does not sin. Always believe and preach this." Refuted: This confuses the "new man" of provision that God graciously provides through Christ for every Christian, with the Christian HIMSELF (see Bonar's "God's Way of Holiness"). But the Christian himself must PUT ON this new man, daily: Ephesians 4:24 And that ye put on the new man, which after God is created in righteousness and true holiness. When the Christian sins, it was the Christian himself who sinned! He sinned by not putting on the new man (God's grace in sanctification through Christ): Hebrews 12:28 ...let us HAVE GRACE, whereby we may serve God acceptably with reverence and godly fear: It will not be the "new nature" that will get the stripes at the Judgment Seat of Christ. It will be the Christian (a saved, justified, blood-bought saint who had a new man of power to walk in, but did not avail himself of it) who will be ashamed and chastised (2 Corinthians 5:9-11). X. "My fellowship with Jesus is not hurt by my sin. Always believe and preach this." Refuted: This lie confuses relationship with fellowship. It confuses the eternal relationship (which cannot be lost by sin) with the daily fellowship (which can be lost). John writes against this lie. He teaches that fellowship is maintained by obedience: 1 John 1:6 If we say that we have fellowship with him, and walk in darkness, we lie, and do not the truth: 7 But if we walk in the light, as he is in the light, we have fellowship one with another, and the blood of Jesus Christ his Son cleanseth us from all sin. Conclusion These ten commandments of libertinism are only a summary. Some Christian libertines have invented many others. Most modern libertines can quote these commandments, and switch from one to the other, and back again, faster than lightning when convicted. May the Lord stir up the hearts of a multitude of preachers to confound these lies, that believers may be fully set free from the dominion and bondage of sin (Romans 6:14, 2 Peter 2:19).
