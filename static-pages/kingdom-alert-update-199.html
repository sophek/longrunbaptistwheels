There is, of course, an attempt to "sanctify" this devilish pride and blasphemy through sophistry, smooth words, and misapplied Scripture. Yet, the Devil's devices are not difficult to detect. They are clearly manifested by the Spirit of God in the third chapter of the first Book of the Holy Bible: Genesis 3:1 Now the SERPENT was more subtil than any beast of the field which the LORD God had made. The final lie (i,e. bait) of the "old serpent" (Revelation 20:2) is that men may become "as gods." However, the Devil does not BEGIN with this lie. He must FIRST set about to destroy the AUTHORITY of God's Word. His first words to Eve were, "Yea, hath God said...?" In other words, the doctrines of the inspiration, the perfect preservation, and the sufficiency of Scripture must be attacked and maligned. After a few generations of assault on Scripture, the Devil launches his next attack: "Ye shall not surely die." Once the Scripture is no longer fully trusted, Satan can tempt man to trust in his own understanding or emotions. The teaching of eternal torment is then attacked. For believers, the doctrine of the Judgment Seat of Christ and the warnings to believers are explained away or ignored. Before long, a majority of unbelievers and believers no longer believe in the inspired, preserved Scriptures (sufficient for every spiritual need), and they no longer FEAR God! Libertinism invades the churches. A mystical, subjective "love" replaces loving obedience to God. Fearing God is seen as bondage and legalism instead of a foundation for true wisdom and power. With these two attacks of the Devil, mankind is then ready to listen to the Devil slander God's goodness: "For God doth know..." In other words, God's commandment is grievous (1 John 5:3). The Devil implies that God is holding some good back from His people. At this point, the victims of the Devil are prepared to hear his ultimate lie: "...in the day ye eat thereof, then your eyes shall be opened, and YE SHALL BE AS GODS..." This lie of deification or divinization is the MAIN (though not only) doctrine of the present falling away. It will, no doubt, continue to grow in popularity. (Benny Hinn, TBN, Dec 1, 1990) Objections Answered When the Bible calls Satan the "god" of this world (2 Corinthians 4:4), it does not mean that Satan is EQUAL with the only true God. In the same manner, the supposed use of the word "god" as a title of some limited authority (as in "lord"), NEVER implies (except in the case of Jesus Christ) that a man or angel is of the same substance or essence with God! It never implies that a man or angel is equal with God! Therefore, to speak of "deification" and "divinization" is blasphemous. When men and angels walk contrary to God's will, they have rebelliously declared themselves to be "gods." They have rebelliously stolen the right to "know" (decide apart from God) "good and evil." But when a man (as Adam and Eve) makes up his own morality, he has NOT become God is essence or substance. He is NOT equal with God in His Divine attributes. He is simply "like" God in the sense that he is INDEPENDENTLY declaring what is good or evil for himself (John 10:34, Genesis 3:5). But God cannot forever allow His creatures to walk "as gods" and make up their own morality! Once they begin to rebel in such a fashion, they must be destroyed, banished, or temporarily restrained (Genesis 3:22, 11:6-7). The Bible teaches that every "god" that did NOT make heaven and earth shall perish (Jeremiah 10:10-11). Therefore, the Devil played a Satanic trick on man. There is only ONE God, and He will not share His unique Divinity with His creatures. It is impossible and blasphemous. For men to act as "gods" (create their own morality), or to blasphemously assume the titles, equality, or worship of God, is to bring down the wrath of God upon their heads! Finally, often the wording of 2 Peter 1:4 is used to justify blasphemy: "Whereby are given unto us exceeding great and precious promises: that by these ye might be PARTAKERS OF the DIVINE nature..." First of all, the phrase "partakers of" does not mean that a person becomes that which he partakes of! Those who will be "partakers of Christ" (Hebrews 3:14) will not BECOME Christ! We that are "partakers of" the Holy Ghost (Hebrews 6:4) have not become the Holy Ghost! The farmer that must first "partake of" his fruits (2 Timothy 2:6) does not BECOME wheat and barley! The phrase obviously means that obedient Christians receive qualities and attributes (nature) in their lives that are given by God (divine) and approved by Him (godliness). Or, it means that "the divine nature" (God), by His grace, freely gives to believers the power to live holy. This is taught in the previous verse: 2 Peter 1:3 According as his DIVINE POWER hath GIVEN unto us all things that pertain unto life and godliness, through the knowledge of him that hath called us to glory and virtue: But it certainly does not teach the blasphemy that Christians are part of the Godhead, have become a member of the Trinity, and are equal in substance and essence with God Himself. It provides no ground for a Satanic "pep rally" wherein thousands of people chant that they are "little gods" and have become "Christ"! It is blasphemous to use the Scriptures that speak of our union and fellowship with Jesus as a grounds for Luciferic presumption!
