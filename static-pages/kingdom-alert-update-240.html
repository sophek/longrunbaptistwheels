We should "abound more and more" in holiness. We should not be stagnant. To be fixed in regard to holiness, means to be fixed in a way that a tree is fixed! It has deep roots. It is not easily shaken with the winds of temptation. It is not easily moved in the storms of life. But although it is fixed and established, it is not lifeless and stagnant. It is growing toward the sun!: 1 Corinthians 15:58 Therefore, my beloved brethren, be ye stedfast, UNMOVEABLE, always ABOUNDING in the work of the Lord, forasmuch as ye know that your labour is not in vain in the Lord. These two things are often found together in Scripture. The man who is rooted and planted and established and fixed IN THE LORD'S WILL, is the man who is ABOUNDING and FLOURISHING: Colossians 2:7 ROOTED and built up in him, and stablished in the faith, as ye have been taught, ABOUNDING therein with thanksgiving. Psalms 92:13 Those that BE PLANTED in the house of the LORD shall FLOURISH in the courts of our God. Proverbs 14:11 The house of the wicked shall be overthrown: but the tabernacle of the upright shall FLOURISH. God wants you to be where you should be in body, soul and spirit; and we know we are where we need to be because we can feel the growing pains! We can feel the challenge. So, as a plant that is deep rooted, and in the sun, will flourish, so should we walk in the light of the Lord's Word. In verse 2 of 1 Thessalonians 4, Paul reminds them that they already know what commandments they have been given by Jesus. And he goes on to remind them of one of the MAIN commandments Christian GENTILES need to hear!: 1 Thessalonians 4:3 For this is the will of God, even your sanctification, that ye should ABSTAIN FROM FORNICATION: 4 That every one of you should know how to possess his vessel in sanctification and honour; 5 Not in the lust of concupiscence, EVEN AS THE GENTILES which know not God: "Concupiscence" means "any unlawful lust or desire." Paul is at this point going directly against MANY CENTURIES of Gentile culture and custom! If it could ever be said, "But everybody does it," it could certainly be said so of fornication at that time! Thessalonica was in Greece: "Thessalonica: Ancient name for Salonica, a Greek seaport on the Aegean Sea. St. Paul's epistles to the Thessalonians were addressed to the Christians in this city." (Grolier Encyclopedia) These ancient Gentiles were "accustomed" to idolatry and fornication: 1 Corinthians 12:2 Ye know that ye were Gentiles, carried away unto these dumb idols, even as ye were led. 1 Corinthians 6:15 Know ye not that your bodies are the members of Christ? shall I then take the members of Christ, and make them the members of AN HARLOT? God forbid. 16 What? know ye not that he which is joined to an harlot is one body? for two, saith he, shall be one flesh. 17 But he that is joined unto the Lord is one spirit. 18 FLEE FORNICATION. Every sin that a man doeth is without the body; but he that committeth fornication sinneth against his own body. 19 What? know ye not that your body is the temple of the Holy Ghost which is in you, which ye have of God, and ye are not your own? The Grecians had been fornicators for centuries: "Even the greatest of heathen moralists, Socrates, instructed a harlot how she should conduct her shameful business. The heathen moralists condemned unchastity only in the case of a childbearing wife, as it would wrong her husband not to know the paternity of her children." ("People's NT Com.") Let us notice some of the history of the ancient Greeks and we can begin to see what many are trying to "bless" us with today!: Sparta (the military state): "...not only must every child face the father's right to infanticide, but it must also be brought before a state council of inspectors; and any child that appeared defective was thrown from a cliff...Husbands were encouraged to lend [out] their wives...so fine children might be multiplied....At the age of seven the Spartan boy was taken from his family and brought up by the state [in a rigorous military regiment]....At twelve the boy was deprived of underclothing, and was allowed but one garment throughout the year....he did not bathe frequently....he slept in the open...Until he was thirty he lived with his company in barracks, and knew none of the comforts of home....He was taught, in preparation for war, to forage in the fields and find his own food, or starve....The girl, though left to be brought up at home, was also subject to regulation by the state...She [must] go naked in public dances and processions...so that she might be stimulated [or shamed] into the proper care of her body....NEARLY EVERY LAD HAD A LOVER AMONG OLDER MEN...[until he married a women at age thirty]. Celibacy in Sparta was a crime....Spartan women, says Plutarch, 'were bold and masculine, overbearing to their husbands...'....if a man's belly swelled indecently he might be publicly reproved by the government....[This] Spartan code produced good soldiers and nothing more....When she fell, all nations marveled, but none mourned. Today, among the scanty ruins of that ancient capital, hardly a torso or a fallen pillar survives to declare that here there once lived Greeks." (Will Durant, "The Life of Greece," 1939) They were shocked when Sparta fell. But the men had become effeminate and perverted, against nature. This is the "culture" people want to bring to us today. In many ways, it is certainly already upon us. The military fornicators exposed in Iraq are a symptom of our Hollywood-addicted culture back home. A nation of fornicators can be CONTROLLED by manipulators. But it is a WEAK society. It will crumble. Let us now move on to notice the state of Corinth: Corinth: "Courtesans [prostitutes] were so numerous in the city that the Greeks often used 'corinthiazomai' as signifying harlotry....The Temple of Aphrodite,' says Strabo, 'was so rich that it owned more than a thousand temple slaves, courtesans [prostitutes] whom both men and women had dedicated to the goddess. And therefore it was on account of these women that the city was crowded with people and grew rich; for instance, the ship captains freely squandered there money here.'" (Will Durant, "The Life of Greece," 1939) Athens: "Premarital chastity is required of respectable women, but among unmarried men...there are few moral restraints upon desire....No stigma is attached to the occasional intercourse of young men with courtesans; even married men may patronize them without any greater penalty than a scolding at home and a slightly tarnished reputation in the city...The lowest order [of harlots was the] pornai...in common brothels...Higher than these girls...are the auletrides, or flute-players, who...assist at 'stag' entertainments, provide music and gaiety, perform dances...[etc.]...The highest class of Greek courtesans is composed of the hetairai - [hi-ter'-ah] literally companions....the hetairai are usually women of the citizen class, who have fallen from respectability...they live independently, and entertain at their own homes....they dye their hair yellow....The chief rivals of the hetairai are the boys of Athens....When Plato...talks of human love, he means homosexual love....[and most of the courtesans are homosexual].....Besides his wife a man may take a concubine. 'We have courtesans for the sake of pleasure,' says Demosthenes, 'concubines for the daily health of our bodies, and wives to bear us lawful offspring and be the faithful guardians of our homes.' here in one startling sentence is the Greek view of woman in the classic age....The law makes adultery by woman, or by a man with a married woman, punishable with death, but the Greeks are too lenient to concupiscence to enforce this statute. The injured husband is usually left to deal with the adulterer as he will and can....For the man divorce is simple; he may simply dismiss his wife at any time, without stating the cause....the children [always, regardless of the cause of divorce] remain with the man...." (Will Durant, "The Life of Greece," 1939) There you have it. And Paul warned us that PERILOUS TIMES were coming (2 Timothy 3:1). Certainly, in these modern times, families are disintegrating and testimonies are being ruined by adultery and fornication. We are commanded to FLEE this sin (and everything that makes provision for it): ABSTAIN BY FLEEING! 1 Thessalonians 4:3 For this is the will of God, even your sanctification, that ye should abstain from fornication: 1 Peter 2:11 Dearly beloved, I beseech you as strangers and pilgrims, abstain from fleshly lusts, which war against the soul; 1 Corinthians 6:18 FLEE fornication. Every sin that a man doeth is without the body; but he that committeth fornication sinneth against his own body. 2 Timothy 2:20 But in a great house there are not only vessels of gold and of silver, but also of wood and of earth; and some to honour, and some to dishonour. 21 If a man therefore purge himself FROM THESE, he shall be a vessel unto honour, sanctified, and meet for the master's use, and prepared unto every good work. 22 FLEE also youthful lusts: but follow righteousness, faith, charity, peace, with them that call on the Lord out of a pure heart. We are to purge ourselves from certain PEOPLE (vessels of dishnour). How can we do this when we bring them into our living rooms by worldly media and entertainment? To "flee" means to RUN, like a deer runs from a hunter: Proverbs 6:5 Deliver thyself as a roe from the hand of the hunter... We need to ESCAPE. That means UNPLUG THE ANTENNA. Escape from STRANGE WOMEN on the screen (or in person): Ecclesiastes 7:26 And I find more bitter than death the woman, whose heart is snares and nets, and her hands as bands: whoso pleaseth God shall ESCAPE FROM HER; but the sinner shall be taken by her. We need to run like Joseph!: Genesis 39:12 And she caught him by his garment, saying, Lie with me: and he left his garment in her hand, and FLED, and got him out. Proverbs 5:8 REMOVE THY WAY FAR FROM HER, and come not nigh the door of her house: Proverbs 7:7 And beheld among the simple ones, I discerned among the youths, a young man void of understanding, 8 Passing through the street near her corner; and he went THE WAY to her house, 9 In the twilight, in the evening, in the black and dark night: 10 And, behold, there met him a woman with the attire of an harlot, and subtil of heart. 11 (She is loud and stubborn; her feet abide not in her house: 22 He GOETH AFTER HER straightway, as an ox goeth to the slaughter, or as a fool to the correction of the stocks; We can survive in the midst of a perverse world only by guarding our hearts and eyes and taking prudent precautions to protect our families.
